Surgeons and patients are increasingly favoring procedures that promise quicker recovery times and reduced postoperative complications, further propelling the adoption of eye stents. <p><p>The Eye Stent market is categorized primarily into two types: Polymer and Gelatin. Polymer eye stents are made from biocompatible materials that provide durability and stability, ensuring effective drainage of intraocular fluid. They are often designed for long-term use. On the other hand, Gelatin eye stents are biodegradable and are utilized for short-term therapeutic purposes. These stents gradually dissolve within the eye, minimizing the need for surgical removal while offering temporary relief from conditions like glaucoma.</p></p>
