<p><p>HVAC systems can be categorized into decentralized and centralized market types. Decentralized systems operate independently, with individual units installed in each space, providing localized control and flexibility. They are commonly used in residential and small commercial settings. In contrast, centralized systems utilize a single, central unit to regulate temperature and air quality throughout a larger area. This approach is often more efficient for larger buildings, allowing for streamlined maintenance and uniform climate control across multiple spaces.</p></p>
