<p><p>The car windshield market features two primary types of materials: thermoplastic and thermoset. Thermoplastic materials, such as polycarbonate, are flexible and can be reshaped with heat, making them lightweight and easier to manufacture. In contrast, thermoset materials, like epoxy or polyurethane, are durable and resistant to heat, providing a strong, permanent shape once cured. Both types serve distinct functions in vehicle design, impacting factors like safety, visibility, and overall weight, influencing consumer choice and industry trends.</p></p>
