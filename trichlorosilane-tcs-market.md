<p><p>Trichlorosilane (TCS) is a key chemical used in the production of high-purity silicon for semiconductor and solar industries. The market for TCS production is primarily divided into two processes: Direct Chlorination (DC) and Hydrochlorination (HC). </p><p>The DC process involves reacting silicon with chlorine gas under high temperatures to yield TCS directly. In contrast, the HC process reacts silicon with hydrogen chloride, providing a more controlled and environmentally friendly method. Both processes cater to the increasing demand for high-quality silicon products.</p></p>
